# Strength Foundations

Scope:
Healthy adults can improve strength with a small set of compound exercises performed consistently. The main design priority is technical quality, repeatable weekly exposure, and gradual load progression. Plans should avoid aggressive loading jumps when a user is new or has limited equipment.

Loading:
For general strength development, sets of four to six repetitions on primary lifts can be effective when the effort stays controlled. Most healthy adults tolerate two to four hard sets per main movement pattern. Beginners benefit from leaving several repetitions in reserve and repeating movement practice before trying heavier loading.

Exercise Selection:
Use stable squat, hinge, push, and pull patterns that match the available equipment. If a movement is limited by discomfort or restriction, switch to a safer variant while preserving the same broad pattern. Consistency across several weeks matters more than novelty.
