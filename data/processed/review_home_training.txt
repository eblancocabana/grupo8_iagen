# Home Training

Equipment Reality:
Home-based training can still cover squat, hinge, push, pull, and trunk patterns with dumbbells, bands, and bodyweight. Exercise selection should emphasize simple setup and stable positions. Single-leg work and floor-based pressing are often practical when heavier equipment is unavailable.

Programming:
Moderate repetition ranges are useful because home loads may be limited. Progression can come from extra repetitions, slower tempo, or an additional set before a large load increase. Home plans should avoid depending on niche machines or highly technical lifts.

Conservative Advice:
When the equipment list is narrow, it is better to repeat a few reliable movements than to invent unusual exercises. Substitutions should keep the same broad pattern and the same conservative intent.
