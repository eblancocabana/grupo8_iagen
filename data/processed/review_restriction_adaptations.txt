# Conservative Adaptations

Overhead Restriction:
If overhead pressing is not tolerated, horizontal pressing or landmine-like angles are often easier to manage. The plan should avoid forcing end-range overhead work simply to keep variety.

Knee and Impact Restriction:
If deep knee flexion or high impact is limited, use box squats, partial range sit-to-stands, step-ups, or low-impact conditioning. Depth and tempo should remain symptom guided. High-impact intervals are not required for a general plan.

Lumbar Restriction:
When loaded lumbar flexion is a concern, choose neutral-spine hinge patterns, glute bridges, and trunk stability work. The goal is to preserve training exposure while reducing obvious mechanical conflict.
