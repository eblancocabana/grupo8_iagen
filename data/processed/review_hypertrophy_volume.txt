# Hypertrophy Volume

Dose Response:
Muscle gain is supported by repeated weekly exposure and moderate volume. A practical starting point for many adults is two to four sets per exercise, with multiple patterns trained each week. Volume should rise slowly when recovery and technique remain acceptable.

Repetition Zones:
A broad repetition range can support hypertrophy, but moderate ranges such as six to twelve repetitions are easy to coach and fit well with simple equipment. Accessory work can use slightly higher repetitions if the movement remains controlled and pain free.

Programming Notes:
Variety is useful when it helps adherence and covers the main movement patterns. It is not necessary to maximize exercise variety inside a single short plan. Stable exercises with clear substitutions are preferable for a four-week prototype.
