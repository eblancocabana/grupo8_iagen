# General Health Training

Scope:
For general health, resistance training should be sustainable, moderate, and easy to recover from. A plan does not need highly specialized loading. It should help the user train regularly, move through common patterns, and keep discomfort low.

Weekly Design:
Two to four sessions per week can work well when each session covers major movement patterns with moderate effort. Technique, breathing, and adherence are more important than chasing maximal effort. Conditioning should stay low impact when the user reports joint concerns.

Session Structure:
Short warmups, a few multi-joint exercises, one or two trunk movements, and simple progression are usually sufficient. When time is limited, trimming accessories is preferable to rushing the main exercises.
